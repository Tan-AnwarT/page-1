Home Techniques Training

Every fighter is different. Body type, height, weight, and natural athleticism all play a role in how you should train.

Step 1: Assess Your Fitness

  • Endurance: Count max push-ups, squats, burpees in 1 minute.
  • Stamina: Time your 1 km run or try 3 minutes of jump rope.
  • Flexibility: Test splits, hamstring reach, and shoulder mobility.
  • Strength baseline: How many pull-ups or dips can you do with proper form?

Step 2: Create Your Workout Plan

  • Strength: Push-ups, pull-ups, squats, lunges.
  • Cardio: Jump rope, shadow boxing, sprints.
  • Explosiveness: Box jumps, plyometric push-ups.

Example weekly split:
Day 1: Strength | Day 2: Cardio | Day 3: Explosive | Day 4: Recovery

Step 3: Train Consistently

  • Schedule 3–5 training days per week.
  • Keep a training journal to track reps.
  • Gradually increase intensity.

Step 4: Stay Motivated

Motivation fades, but discipline keeps you going.
